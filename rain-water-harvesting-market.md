<p><p>Rainwater harvesting markets are primarily categorized into above-ground and underground systems. Above-ground systems involve capturing rainwater in barrels or tanks, making it easily accessible for irrigation and other uses. These systems are typically simpler to install and maintain. In contrast, underground systems utilize cisterns or storage tanks buried below the surface, offering a more discreet and space-efficient solution. Underground systems are often preferred for larger-scale applications and provide better protection from contamination and evaporation while maximizing available space.</p></p>
