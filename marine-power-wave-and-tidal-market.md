<p><p>Marine power encompasses energy derived from oceanic waves and tidal movements, classified into two main market types: wave power and tidal power. Wave power harnesses energy from surface waves generated by wind, utilizing devices like buoys and oscillating water columns to convert kinetic energy into electricity. Tidal power exploits the gravitational pull of the moon and sun, employing turbines and barrages in tidal streams or estuaries. Both markets aim to contribute to renewable energy goals by providing sustainable alternatives to fossil fuels.</p></p>
