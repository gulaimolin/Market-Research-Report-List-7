<p><p>Tunnel diodes are categorized into heavily doped N-type and heavily doped P-type markets based on their doping concentration. Heavily doped N-type tunnel diodes have excess electrons, enhancing electron tunneling, which makes them efficient for high-speed applications and oscillators. Conversely, heavily doped P-type tunnel diodes contain excess holes, enabling similar tunneling effects but with different operational characteristics. Both types are vital in niche applications like oscillators, amplifiers, and switching devices due to their unique voltage-current characteristics and fast response times.</p></p>
