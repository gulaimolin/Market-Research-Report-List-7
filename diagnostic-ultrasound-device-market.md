<p><p>The diagnostic ultrasound device market is categorized into black and white (B/W) and color ultrasound systems. Black and white ultrasound, also known as 2D ultrasound, provides basic imaging using grayscale to depict structures and organs. It is widely used for routine examinations due to its affordability and simplicity. In contrast, color ultrasound employs Doppler technology to visualize blood flow and vascular structures in real-time, enabling more detailed assessments. This features enhanced diagnostic capabilities, making it vital for various clinical applications.</p></p>
