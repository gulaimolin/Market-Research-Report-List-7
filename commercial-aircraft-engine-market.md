<p><p>The commercial aircraft engine market includes four main types: Turboprop engines combine a gas turbine with a propeller, ideal for short-haul regional flights due to fuel efficiency. Turbofan engines, with a large fan at the front, dominate long-haul commercial aviation, offering high thrust and reduced noise. Turboshaft engines power helicopters, delivering high torque for rotor systems. Piston engines, using reciprocating pistons, are common in small, general aviation aircraft for their simplicity and cost-effectiveness. Each type serves specific operational needs in the aviation industry.</p></p>
